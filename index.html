Media I Use

Content Consumption

Media plays a huge role in my daily life. Here’s how I consume content across different platforms:

Music: My go-to genre is K-pop. I love the vibrant performances and catchy melodies of groups like Twice, Blackpink, and EXO. Whether I’m working or relaxing, music is my constant companion, and I often discover new artists through Spotify and YouTube playlists.

Games: I enjoy a mix of games depending on my mood. For open-world exploration, I play Genshin Impact, where the stunning visuals and intricate storylines keep me engaged. When I’m looking for a quick challenge, I turn to puzzle games like Minesweeper that test my logical thinking skills.

Pictures: Visual content like digital art, memes, and photography inspire me. I often scroll through Instagram and Pinterest to admire the creativity of artists and photographers, and I save ideas that spark my own creativity.

Videos: My video preferences range from entertaining TV shows to educational tutorials. I love binge-watching shows like Brooklyn Nine-Nine for its humor and unique characters. For deeper insights, I explore documentaries like those on Rotten Tomatoes, and for hobbies, I watch painting tutorials to improve my skills.

Challenges with Media Tools

While media tools empower creativity and connectivity, they come with their own set of challenges. For instance, mastering software like Photoshop or advanced video editing platforms requires a steep learning curve. I often find myself dedicating significant time to learning the nuances of these tools, but the results are rewarding.

Another challenge is managing the overwhelming variety of content available for consumption. Platforms like YouTube and Netflix often lead to unintentional binge-watching, making it difficult to maintain focus and prioritize my time. I’m working on being more intentional with my media choices to ensure they align with my personal and professional goals.
